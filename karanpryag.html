Karnaprayag Sangam

Location: Karnaprayag lies in the Chamoli district of Uttarakhand, where the rivers Alaknanda and Pindar meet.

Significance: This sacred confluence is one of the Panch Prayag, a place where devotees perform holy rituals and prayers. Mythology connects it with Karna from the Mahabharata, who meditated here to worship the Sun God and gain divine armor.

Scenic Beauty: Surrounded by majestic mountains and serene rivers, it is a tranquil spot for reflection and meditation.

Karna Mandir

Dedicated to: Karna, the great warrior from the Mahabharata.

The temple near the Sangam is one of the few in India dedicated to Karna. Pilgrims visit it to honor his generosity, loyalty, and unmatched skills as a warrior. Special prayers are held during festivals.

Uma Devi Temple

Dedicated to: Goddess Parvati (Uma).

This ancient temple reflects traditional Himalayan design and is a revered site for its spiritual energy. Devotees visit throughout the year to seek blessings.

Nearby Attractions

  • Nauti Village: About 25 km from Karnaprayag, this village is the starting point of the famous Nanda Devi Raj Jat Yatra.
  • Adi Badri Temple: A group of 16 ancient temples dedicated to Lord Vishnu, located 19 km from Karnaprayag.
  • Chandika Devi Temple: Dedicated to Goddess Chandika, this is another prominent site for pilgrims.
  • Treks: Explore nearby treks like Kauri Pass, Roopkund, and the Valley of Flowers.

Best Time to Visit

  • Summer (April to June): Pleasant weather for sightseeing and pilgrimages.
  • Monsoon (July to September): Lush greenery but potential landslides may disrupt travel.
  • Winter (October to March): Cold and serene, ideal for those seeking tranquility.
